The Risk Vectors of Urban Rodents

Attics, crawlspaces, and drywall compartments are favorite targets for invasive rodents like Norway rats, roof rats, and house mice. Beyond damaging structural support beams and chewing through insulation, rodents carry pathogens that pose serious public health concerns.

Electrical Fires

Rodents incessantly chew wire insulation, provoking dangerous short circuits.

Pathogen Vectors

Leptospirosis, Hantavirus, and Salmonellosis are spread via waste products.

The 3 Pillars of Professional Rodent Exclusion

DIY containment can fail when rodent entries remain unrecognized. Follow these structural sealing pillars to secure your building's integrity permanently.

1. Thorough Perimeter Scan

Weep holes, crawlspace vents, and dryer ducts represent the primary entry routes for nesting pests. Systematically mark and document all crawlspace entry ways.

2. Hardware Cloth Sealing

Standard expanding foam is easily bypassed by sharp incisors. Utilize heavy-duty steel mesh screen or copper wool mesh packed alongside strong masonry caulks.

3. Attraction Reduction

Trim back tree canopies at least 6 feet away from your rooflines. Keep all dry pet food and garbage cans in airtight, hard-plastic bins.

Frequently Asked Pest Questions

Find clarity on the most common residential exclusion questions.

How small of a hole can a mouse pass through?

A standard house mouse can squeeze through any gap or crack the size of a dime (approx. 1/4 inch). Rats can pass through a opening the size of a quarter. This is why thorough exclusion using steel screens or copper mesh is highly essential.

Why are over-the-counter rodent baits often risky?

Baits can lead to rodents dying hidden inside drywall compartments, creating lingering odors and sanitation problems. Additionally, open rodent baits pose secondary poisoning hazards to neighborhood pets and predatory local wildlife.

When should I engage professional extermination services?

If you hear regular scratching noises inside wall spaces, notice dark droppings in kitchen pantry shelves, or suspect electrical chewing, you should call a certified professional to run a precise exclusion and bait sequence immediately.
